# Redesign Retrospective

Scope: `full_redesign_*` and `full_redesign_stage2e70_*`

This note summarizes what changed in the redesign, what the experiments actually showed, what is working, what is not working, why the observed behavior makes sense technically, and what the next steps should be.

The conclusions here are based on:

- `runs/full_redesign_*/.../evaluation/evaluation_metrics.json`
- `runs/full_redesign_stage2e70_*/.../evaluation/evaluation_metrics.json`
- `training_summary.json` and `training_history.json` for both families
- `evaluation/cluster_diagnostics/*_summary.json`
- `runs/full_redesign_stage2e70_timings.json`
- the current implementation in [project/main.py](project/main.py), [project/train.py](project/train.py), [project/visualize_pipeline.py](project/visualize_pipeline.py), [project/test/legacy_analysis/analyze_cluster_diagnostics.py](project/test/legacy_analysis/analyze_cluster_diagnostics.py), and the redesign config files

## 1. What Changed

The redesign family introduced a different anchor pipeline and a more observable training loop.

Core redesign choices:

- anchors are generated by k-means in frozen DINOv3 embedding space
- anchors use `representation: centroids` instead of nearest real samples
- anchors are pruned after clustering with `discard_ratio: 0.2` for sufficiently large K
- anchors remain stored in semantic space and are reprojected each forward pass with `reproject_anchors: true`
- stage-1 pseudo-labels are fixed and capacity-constrained with `pseudo_label_assignment: capacitated` and `capacity_multiplier: 2.0`
- stage-2 aligns to the sample's fixed anchor label with `alignment_target: anchor`
- repeller loss remained active with `beta: 0.5`
- assignment-diversity loss was disabled with `delta: 0.0`

The later `full_redesign_stage2e70` family kept the same redesign choices and changed two things:

- stage-1 early stopping cannot trigger before epoch 20 via `training.min_epochs_before_early_stopping: 20`
- stage-2 epoch budget was raised from 20 to 70

Important implementation consequences:

- `training_summary.json` is stage-1-only and does not summarize stage-2
- final evaluation prefers `best_stage2_model.pth` when it exists
- the `pipeline_viz` `step04` plots show the trained projection space at evaluation time, not the raw 384D space
- `step04` includes both normal and anomalous samples; the plain plots separate them by color and the cluster plots separate them by marker shape

## 2. Headline Findings

1. The redesign avoided catastrophic one-anchor collapse, but it did not solve the stage-1 model-selection problem for `K > 1`.
2. For `K = 4`, the model shows the cleanest cluster structure and strongest assignment confidence, but not the best final anomaly AUROC.
3. For `K = 1024`, the model achieves the best final fused image AUROC, but the anchor usage pattern is highly fragmented and per-sample assignments are weakly separated.
4. Extending stage-2 to a 70-epoch budget helped mainly at `K = 1024`; it did not produce broad gains across all K.
5. Forcing stage-1 to train at least 20 epochs did not move the best stage-1 checkpoint away from epoch 1 for `K = 4, 16, 256, 1024`.
6. The new diagnostics were necessary. AUROC alone would not have shown the large-K underuse and assignment ambiguity.

### 3.3 Net Effect of `stage2e70`

The `stage2e70` changes were not a universal improvement.

- `K = 1024` improved clearly in fused AUROC: `0.7869 -> 0.8260`
- `K = 1` and `K = 4` improved slightly in fused AUROC
- `K = 16` became slightly worse in fused AUROC
- `K = 256` was effectively flat
- image AUROC changed very little overall; the main visible movement was in fused and pixel-side signals

Interpretation:

- the extra stage-2 budget mainly helped where the larger anchor bank gave stage-2 more useful residual structure to exploit
- the stage-1 min-epoch floor did not itself fix stage-1 selection for `K > 1`

## 5. Cluster Diagnostics: What They Mean

The post-hoc diagnostics in [project/test/legacy_analysis/analyze_cluster_diagnostics.py](project/test/legacy_analysis/analyze_cluster_diagnostics.py) define:

- `d1`: mean nearest-anchor distance
- `d2`: mean second-nearest-anchor distance
- `margin`: `d2 - d1`
- `ratio`: `d1 / d2`
- `effective_anchors_used`: how many anchors received at least one sample
- `largest_anchor_share`: fraction of samples assigned to the busiest anchor
- `normalized_entropy`: how spread the assignment histogram is

How to read them:

- lower `d1` is better for normals
- larger `margin` is better because the winning anchor is clearly better than the runner-up
- lower `ratio` is better because the chosen anchor is much closer than the second-best anchor
- higher `effective_anchors_used` and entropy indicate broader usage, but broad usage alone is not enough if margins remain tiny

## 6. What the Diagnostics Say

### 6.1 Small K and Large K Behave Differently

For validation normals in `full_redesign_stage2e70`:

| K | Effective K After Prune | Anchors Used | Normal d1 | Normal d2 | Margin | Ratio |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| 1 | 1 | 1 | 0.0042 | - | - | - |
| 4 | 4 | 4 | 0.5164 | 1.1104 | 0.5940 | 0.4792 |
| 16 | 13 | 9 | 0.4899 | 0.6373 | 0.1474 | 0.7694 |
| 256 | 205 | 32 | 0.5077 | 0.5431 | 0.0354 | 0.9325 |
| 1024 | 820 | 36 | 0.5159 | 0.5468 | 0.0309 | 0.9408 |

This is one of the central findings.

`K = 4` has the cleanest assignment structure:

- all 4 anchors are used on validation normals
- the margin is large
- the ratio is low
- assignments are comparatively confident

As K grows:

- `d1` does not improve much
- `d2` gets very close to `d1`
- margin collapses toward zero
- ratio moves close to 1

That means large K does not create clean, decisive clusters. It creates many nearly interchangeable nearby anchors.

### 6.2 Large K Is Sparse, Not Fully Utilized

For `K = 1024` in `full_redesign_stage2e70`:

- requested `K = 1024`
- effective `K = 820` after pruning
- validation normals use only `36` anchors
- test normals use `245` anchors

This is not one-anchor collapse. The model does not collapse into a single dominant anchor. Instead, it fragments into a very large bank of lightly used anchors with weak local separation.

That has two consequences:

- it can still help final anomaly scoring because the anchor bank gives fine-grained coverage
- it is poor if the goal is interpretable, confident cluster structure

### 6.3 Good Clustering and Best AUROC Are Not the Same Goal

`K = 4` is the best clustering story.

- strong margins
- full usage
- low ratio
- readable step-4 cluster plots

But `K = 4` is not the best final anomaly detector by AUROC.

`K = 1024` is the opposite story.

- weak assignment margins
- fragmented usage
- less interpretable cluster structure
- best final fused image AUROC in the current redesign families

This tension is important. The current pipeline is not optimizing a single thing called "good clustering." It is trading off:

- one-class anomaly separation
- anchor-bank expressiveness
- stage-2 reconstruction signal
- cluster interpretability

## 7. What Is Working

### 7.1 The redesign anchor pipeline is viable

The centroid-plus-pruning-plus-capacitated-label pipeline works operationally.

- runs complete consistently
- large requested K values reduce to sensible effective K values after pruning
- fixed pseudo-label assignments remain valid throughout training
- post-hoc diagnostics and pipeline plots can inspect the resulting geometry

This is already a substantial improvement over a pipeline where anchor behavior is invisible.

### 7.2 Repeller-on redesign avoids the earlier collapse failure mode

Earlier collapse analysis showed why attractor-only behavior was unstable. The redesign configs do not repeat that exact failure because they keep `beta: 0.5`.

Evidence:

- multi-anchor runs do not degenerate into one dominant anchor owning almost everything
- `K = 4` and `K = 16` show real multi-anchor structure
- even large K runs use many anchors, although weakly

### 7.3 Stage-2 consistently produces strong pixel-level signals

Across both families, pixel AUROC remains strong, roughly `0.916` to `0.933`.

That means the reconstruction branch is not failing wholesale. The main uncertainty is how much that pixel-side signal helps final image-level detection at each K.

### 7.4 `stage2e70` clearly helped at `K = 1024`

This is the strongest result in the new family.

Compared with `full_redesign_k1024`:

- stage-2 trained longer: `16 -> 27` epochs
- best validation pixel-agg AUROC improved: `0.9003 -> 0.9219`
- fused image AUROC improved: `0.7869 -> 0.8260`

The most plausible explanation is that longer stage-2 training gave the large anchor bank a better reconstruction-side signal to combine with the anchor score.

## 8. What Is Not Working

### 8.1 Stage-1 checkpoint selection is still the main unresolved problem

For every `K > 1`, stage-1 best epoch is still epoch 1 in the `stage2e70` family.

That means:

- the added `min_epochs_before_early_stopping` floor changed run length, not stage-1 model quality
- stage-1 `image_auroc` is still not the right main signal if the goal is well-formed anchor structure
- stage-1 is likely optimizing a one-class anomaly proxy that is only loosely related to multi-anchor geometry

### 8.2 Large K remains hard to justify as a clustering story

At `K = 256` and `K = 1024`:

- normal assignments use a small subset of the available anchors on validation
- `d1` and `d2` are extremely close
- margins are tiny
- ratios are near 1

So the model does not learn crisp anchor membership at large K. It learns a dense, weakly separated anchor field.

This may still be useful for anomaly scoring, but it is not evidence that the model discovered semantically strong clusters.

### 8.3 `stage2e70` did not provide a broad win

The new family improved the `K = 1024` result meaningfully, but it was not a universal upgrade.

- `K = 16` fused AUROC decreased
- `K = 256` fused AUROC was flat to slightly worse
- image AUROC was largely unchanged
- stage-2 still early-stopped well before the 70-epoch budget in every run

So the current evidence does not support a general statement that "longer stage-2 is better." It supports a narrower statement: "longer stage-2 helped the largest-K case."

### 8.4 `K = 1` is strong, but not a clustering success

`K = 1` remains a strong anomaly baseline with good image and excellent pixel AUROC. But it does not answer the clustering question because:

- there is no second anchor
- `d2`, margin, and ratio are undefined
- all samples must map to the single anchor by design

It is useful as a one-class center baseline, not as evidence that the redesign solved multi-anchor structure.

## 9. Why the Main Outcomes Make Sense

### 9.1 Why `K = 4` looks structurally best

With a small anchor count, each anchor must represent a larger portion of normal variation.

That encourages:

- stronger competition between anchors
- clearer nearest-vs-second-nearest separation
- more interpretable cluster plots

The cost is lower representational granularity. There may simply not be enough anchors to capture subtle normal modes and boundary cases, which can limit final image AUROC.

### 9.2 Why `K = 1024` scores best but clusters worst

Large K provides a rich anchor bank. That can improve anomaly scoring because a sample can find a nearby normal reference. But once the anchor bank becomes very dense:

- many anchors become near-ties for the same sample
- nearest and second-nearest anchors are extremely similar
- assignment confidence falls even if nearest distance stays reasonable

So large K acts more like a fine-grained reference bank than like a set of well-separated clusters.

### 9.3 Why the stage-1 min-epoch floor was not enough

The floor only prevents an early stop before epoch 20. It does not change what the model is optimizing or how the best checkpoint is selected.

If the monitored metric is still misaligned, the best checkpoint can remain at epoch 1 even though training continues to epoch 20.

That is exactly what happened for all multi-anchor `stage2e70` runs.

### 9.4 Why `stage2e70` helped `K = 1024`

`K = 1024` had the most room for stage-2 to matter.

- the anchor bank was the richest
- the original stage-2 run stopped after 16 epochs
- the extended family reached 27 stage-2 epochs and a much better validation pixel-agg peak

The data suggest that the gain came primarily from stage-2, not from the stage-1 minimum-epoch floor.

## 10. What the New Diagnostics Added

The new observability work mattered.

Useful additions:

- per-epoch stage-1 validation cluster diagnostics on normals
- cluster-colored `pipeline_viz` step-4 plots
- post-hoc [project/test/legacy_analysis/analyze_cluster_diagnostics.py](project/test/legacy_analysis/analyze_cluster_diagnostics.py)
- timing-aware `run_full_redesign_stage2e70_k_experiments.ps1`

Without those additions, several important conclusions would have been invisible:

- stage-1 is still best at epoch 1 for `K > 1`
- large K is sparse and weakly separated rather than fully collapsed
- the 70-epoch stage-2 budget is mostly theoretical because early stopping remains active
- "better AUROC" and "better clustering" are not the same success criterion

## 12. Current Best Baselines

There is not one single "best" redesign run. It depends on the objective.

If the goal is final image-level anomaly performance:

- current best in these families is `full_redesign_stage2e70_k1024`
- best fused image AUROC: `0.8260`

If the goal is the cleanest anchor structure:

- `K = 4` is the clearest current answer
- it has the strongest margin and lowest ratio among the true multi-anchor runs
- it uses all anchors and gives the most interpretable cluster plots

If the goal is a strong simple one-class baseline:

- `K = 1` remains competitive and robust
- but it should not be confused with solving the multi-anchor clustering problem

## 13. Recommended Next Steps

### Priority 1: Change stage-1 checkpoint selection

Do not continue using stage-1 `image_auroc` alone as the main selection signal for multi-anchor runs.

Recommended direction:

- monitor a normals-only clustering metric alongside image AUROC
- candidates: normal `d1`, margin, ratio, effective anchors used, or a small composite score
- keep image AUROC as a secondary guard rather than the only decision signal

This is the most important unresolved issue because the current evidence says stage-1 is still effectively choosing epoch 1 for all multi-anchor runs.

### Priority 2: Decide what large K is supposed to achieve

Right now large K improves final fused AUROC but does not produce crisp cluster structure.

That leads to a design choice:

- if the real objective is best anomaly AUROC, then `K = 1024` remains worth pursuing
- if the real objective is meaningful multi-anchor clustering, then `K = 4` to `K = 16` look more honest and interpretable

This should be decided explicitly, because the current pipeline is optimizing those two goals differently.

### Priority 3: If large K remains important, test ways to sharpen assignments

The main large-K weakness is not nearest distance. It is nearest-vs-second-nearest ambiguity.

Recommended directions:

- try stronger assignment regularization rather than only longer training
- revisit dynamic reassignment or softer label updates for large K only
- consider reducing final effective K more aggressively after pruning
- consider a selection or regularization scheme that directly rewards larger margins or lower `d1 / d2`

### Priority 4: Revisit stage-2 selection if fused image performance is the target

Stage-2 early stopping currently follows `pixel_aggregated_image_auroc`.

That is reasonable for reconstruction quality, but the final user-facing objective is often fused image AUROC, not pixel-agg AUROC alone.

Recommended direction:

- evaluate whether stage-2 checkpoint selection should remain pixel-only
- consider tracking a secondary fused validation metric for selection or for post-hoc checkpoint choice

### Priority 5: Keep the new diagnostics and use them as first-class outputs

The current diagnostics are already useful enough to justify keeping them as part of the normal experiment workflow.

Recommended direction:

- keep generating `cluster_diagnostics`
- keep per-epoch stage-1 cluster logging
- keep the cluster-colored pipeline plots
- treat those outputs as standard evidence when judging future redesign iterations

## 14. Bottom Line

The redesign succeeded in making the anchor pipeline more principled and much more observable. It also produced a stronger final result at `K = 1024` once stage-2 was allowed more room to train.

But the central research question is still open.

The current pipeline can achieve good anomaly AUROC without learning clean, decisive multi-anchor structure. The strongest evidence for that is the combination of:

- epoch-1 best stage-1 checkpoints for every `K > 1`
- strong final `K = 1024` fused AUROC
- very weak `d2 - d1` margins and near-1 `d1 / d2` ratios at large K

So the next iteration should not just ask "can the score go up?" It should ask which objective matters most:

- anomaly detection quality
- interpretable clustering quality
- or a deliberate compromise between the two

That choice should determine the next redesign, because the current evidence says those goals are no longer equivalent.
